Written for: Ione in the Yuletide 2006 Challenge

Yes, I totally was doing the misdirection thing there in the summary, cough cough. So, yes! This was a pinch hit done in the last fifteen minutes before the stroke of midnight, I just whipped it out, and -- okay, that is a lie. Actually this story has been languishing for about 3 years on my hard drive, refusing to end and be posted, and then in the panicked frenzy of Yuletide Madness, I thought of it, and somehow the resolution came to me. Many thanks to damned_colonial for beta on this, lo these many moons ago&44; and to Ione for providing the kick in the rear.



In the warm, dim candlelight, the pale marble might almost have been the skin of a living woman. The man standing by the tomb did not touch the effigy, as if to preserve the illusion for himself; his own face was scarcely less pale than the carved one, and they seemed well-matched in this, although the serene death-mask was beautiful, and his face was not.

"Certainly, if I wished to permit that creature Harte to sell me to the highest bidder, like a piece of cattle," Stephen said.
